Greenhouse gas emissions can be measured through life cycle assessments or other calculation methods such as carbon accounting.
Direct greenhouse gas emissions come from sources owned or controlled by the audited and reporting organization. These emissions measurements are also referred to as the Primary Carbon Footprint.
Energy Indirect greenhouse gas emissions are emissions from sources that are a result of the reporting organization's activities but that are owned or controlled by another legal entity. 
These measurements are also called Secondary Carbon Footprint.
B)
1. Covering incoming direct emissions that turn out to be owned or controlled by a company.
2. Emissions caused by a company as a share of the energy production it imports or buys and uses.
3. It includes all other indirect emissions that occur in the value chain that are not the result of activities from assets not produced, owned or controlled by the company itself.
